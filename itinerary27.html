Itinerary

Day 1: The Ultimate Parent-Daughter Road Trip

Welcome to Holland! We've crafted a special 'Introduction to Amsterdam' to start your journey on the right note. Prepare for an unforgettable intro into the wonders of "Olympic-Gold" hockey and the charm of Amsterdam. We're set. We're ready. Let's go! Join us!

Details for Day 1 Here

We hope you're ready to dive into the adventure awaiting you right from the moment you land.

Upon your arrival, a dedicated staff member and EuroTour bus will be ready at the airport to welcome morning arrivals. For a seamless transition to your adventure, we've organized a group transfer set to depart at 11am. For those arriving outside this window, worry not; our hotel is conveniently located a mere 15-20 minute taxi or Uber ride away.

Once at the hotel, take the opportunity to refresh and change, and enjoy some welcoming snacks prepared just for you. Then, at 1:00pm, join us as we embark on an enthralling introduction to Holland, marking the perfect start to your journey with us.

Without revealing all the surprises we have in store, let's just say you'll unmistakably feel the essence of Holland upon reaching our carefully chosen destination. Immerse yourself in a leisurely afternoon surrounded by iconic Dutch landscapes—think historic windmills and charming green wooden houses that seem lifted from a storybook.

Our journey then leads us to the vibrant heart of Amsterdam, Vondelpark, the largest and most renowned city park in the Netherlands. It's a beloved spot where locals and visitors alike bask in the sun, engage in a variety of outdoor activities, and simply enjoy the lively atmosphere. Here, nestled among the lush greenery, we'll indulge in a fan favorite, a BBQ at one of the park's cozy cafes, making new friends with our EuroTour traveling companions.

As the day winds down, we'll return you to the comfort of your 4-star hotel, where you can relax and reflect on the day's adventures, resting up for a new day of hockey exploration and fun.

HOTEL TIP
Parents, take note: Our accommodation, the Hotel & Wellness Zuiver, isn't just a place to stay; it's one of the premier spa destinations in Holland. With luxurious pools, soothing massages, and a full suite of wellness services, it promises relaxation and rejuvenation. For more details on the indulgent experiences awaiting you, we encourage you to visit their website. HOTEL LINK

Day 2: Masterclass in Motion

Today is a highlight for our athletes! Dedicate this special week to honing your skills with all-day training led by elite FIH coaches. Prepare to be challenged, inspired, and transformed on the field. Mom, dad... this is your vacation too, so enjoy all that our spa hotel and Holland has to offer.

Details for Day 2 Here

ARE YOU READY? We use words like unforgettable, once-in-a-lifetime, and priceless to describe what this week of training holds—and you'll understand why the moment you step on the field.

Day 2 dawns with anticipation—today is a highlight for our athletes! Kickstart your morning with a hearty breakfast that will fuel your day's adventures. Afterward, we'll embark on a refreshing short walk to pick up your bikes, setting the stage for a picturesque ride. Our journey takes us through the serene Amsterdam Forest, pedaling our way to the club and training pitch where you'll meet our esteemed Dutch coaches.

Today's session promises to be an outstanding hockey experience. Prepare to be inspired as you learn new skills and techniques in a way that only the Dutch can offer—making hockey not just a sport but a fun-filled adventure.

For our adult members, the adventure begins in earnest once we've seen the athletes off to their training. We'll guide you to the bike center, where you'll be outfitted with your bike for the week. This isn't just any bike—it's your passport to freedom, allowing you to glide effortlessly to the pitch to cheer on your daughter or venture into the heart of the city for some exploration. The choice is yours, and the possibilities are endless. It's your vacation, after all!

Once you're comfortable with your bikes, the adults—or "fans," as we affectionately call you—will hop on a bus into the city center for an orientation tour. We'll begin with a leisurely walk starting from the Anne Frank House, continuing to Dam Square, strolling through the charming boutiques and shops of the Nine Streets area, and then exploring the vibrant Floating Flower Market. We'll conclude our journey at the beautiful outer canal ring near Leidseplein, returning to the hotel before your daughters finish their training session.

From then on and throughout the week, the freedom to explore is entirely in your hands—whether you prefer to cycle, take the bus, or hail an Uber, you choose your mode of transportation.

This evening, if the weather is on our side, we will all embark on a scenic bike ride to a traditional farmhouse. There, we'll enjoy a special dinner; authentic Dutch pancakes—a perfect ending to a wonderful day.

BIKING TIP
Biking into the city is a breeze, thanks to mostly flat bike paths leading the way. A brief stint on the road brings you to Vondel Park, where you can enjoy a ride through the park right to the city's edge. Once there, it's wise to lock up your bike and continue on foot.
AMSTERDAM ON FOOT
Experiencing Amsterdam is truly best on foot. Its easy-to-navigate layout, guided by the distinctive horseshoe-shaped canal system, makes exploring and orienting yourself a simple pleasure.

Family Shared Experience!

Okay, we get it. There's nothing better than watching your child play hockey. I've been there.

But being part of that experience, and creating new, shared experiences, is something truly unique.

Some of my most cherished memories are from traveling with my two daughters as they played hockey in Holland. Those moments I'll hold onto forever. And they still talk about it fondly too.

Have you looked over our itinerary? You should. Here are just a few of the shared experiences outside of hockey you'll have that will make this trip extremely special for you, too:

Visiting Working Windmills: On our first day you'll step into the heart of Dutch culture as you explore centuries-old windmills that are still in operation. You'll see firsthand how these iconic structures have shaped the country's history and watch traditional techniques in action.

Discovering the Art of Wooden Shoe Making: We'll visit a local workshop where craftsmen keep the art of clog-making alive. It's fascinating to see how a simple block of wood transforms into one of the Netherlands' most recognizable symbols.

Wandering through Amsterdam's Parks: On day one, we'll take you on a leisurely stroll through the sprawling park that frames this vibrant city. Vondelpark is a favorite for Dutch singles, couples and families soaking in the beauty of the surroundings. Awesome people watching.

Exploring Utrecht's Historic Charm: On our day of sightseeing we visit the city of Utrecht. Known for its medieval architecture and winding canals, Utrecht offers a cozy yet vibrant atmosphere. Walk freely down its picturesque pedestrian streets, discovering hidden cafes and boutiques, or enjoy a meal in a canal-side restaurant.

Experiencing the Lively Nightlife: After a day of games and training, head out together for a memorable evening. Whether it's a family dinner at a bustling restaurant, sampling local street food, or taking in the lively city energy, there's something magical about Holland's evening vibe.

Seeing the Canals Come Alive at Night: As the sun sets, the city transforms. The canals, illuminated by soft, glowing lights, create a dreamy backdrop for an evening boat ride or a leisurely walk along the water's edge. This is when you should visit the city and take advantage of the late sunsets here in July.

Indulging in Dutch Pancakes: On day two, we'll take you to a farmhouse that specializes in authentic Dutch pancakes. Thick, fluffy, and served with a variety of toppings—from sweet powdered sugar to savory bacon and cheese—Dutch pancakes are a must-try.

Riding Bikes Together: Join the locals in one of the Netherlands' most beloved pastimes. Bike to grab a late-night snack of ice cream or crispy French fries, or simply pedal through town, taking in the sights and sounds. It's a fun way to experience the city together, just like real Dutchies.

These shared experiences aren't just about sightseeing—they're about bonding, creating memories, and having those special moments together that will last long after the trip is over.

So yes, you'll watch your daughter play, she'll get better, make friends and network with our coaches. But you'll also share an adventure that's as much yours as it is hers. And you'll call it "Ours"!

Bob Whitcher, Founder, Sport EuroTour

As an example: if both parents intend to travel with their daughter, the parents will be assigned a superior room together with one bed and the athlete will be assigned a double room, with another member athlete in a similar position. For parents traveling with their athlete, we will endeavor to pair your athlete with another in a similar situation for room sharing.

Couples Accommodation: Parents & Partners will be placed in a Superior room featuring a full-size bed. Superior Room bed width: 180cm / 70.86 inches / 5'11" wide. That's almost 11 inches wider than a queen – 5.25 inches less than a King.

Single Rooms: Availability of single rooms is limited due to the boutique size and nature of the hotel, which ultimately restricts the number of single rooms available. There is a single room supplement.

Rooming Policy: Per the hotel, there are no triples or three-to-a-room options available.

Pricing Policy: All prices are based on two to a room – double occupancy.

Again, the hotel is small, with limited capacity. Some of our rooms are Standard two-bedded and some Superior with only one large 2-person bed. Rooms will be assigned by first to book, room/bed availability. However, within this hierarchy, 2-bedded room priority will be given to: Guardian & Athlete sharing a room. Parents/couples traveling together as chaperones will be automatically assigned a Superior room with one double bed. If a parent or guardian ends up in a room on their own, by themselves, because of the number of athletes they are chaperoning or family members they are traveling with, they will need to purchase the single supplement. As previously stated, these single rooms are in limited supply.

If two-bedded rooms are unavailable as outlined above, we will discuss alternatives with you. If for any reason you are not satisfied with the bed configuration, we will offer a full refund at the time of booking or within the registration grace period. Please understand we are limited in our options and flexibility.

Bikes & Biking We Ride Like Dutchies

Bikes for Everyone – Just Like the Dutch

In Amsterdam, biking isn't just transportation—it's a way of life. With flat terrain and bike paths everywhere, most Dutch people get around by bike… and so do we!

All athletes and family members will have their own bike for the week. Athletes will ride from the hotel to our training facility—just 1.5 miles each way along a safe, flat path through a park (no cars!).

Family members can use their bikes to visit the pitch, explore the city, and join us for a fun group ride to dinner at the famous Pancake House—about 2 miles away, again on flat bike paths.

If biking isn't for you, that's totally okay! But it will make things a little harder and maybe more expensive. You'll have to walk the 1.5 miles to training or hop in an Uber. Biking isn't mandatory, but it's definitely the best way to experience the local culture—and it's part of the fun of our program.

Note too, we'll also use our private bus when it makes sense: on Day 1 for sightseeing, Day 2 for a city orientation visit for our "fans" (while athletes are at their first day of training), and on our family exploration day to Utrecht.

Straight Answers

Quick Answers for Parents

Does my daughter need to be an elite player?

No. The tour is open to any female field hockey athlete graduating high school in 2028 through 2030. Training groups are 10 or fewer, so our Dutch coaches meet every athlete at her level. We do recommend she's serious about playing in college, has the skills to grow under this level of coaching, and the stamina to bring those skills to a full week of intense training.

Can my daughter travel without a parent?

Every athlete must travel with a parent or an appointed guardian over 21. The guardian can be a family friend, typically another parent traveling with a friend of their daughter, designated by you to be responsible for her during the trip.

Are dads and siblings welcome?

Yes. Spouses, partners, siblings, grandparents, aunts, uncles, and close friends are all welcome. Room availability at our boutique hotel is the only limit, so book family members early.

What does the tour cost and what's included?

Athletes are $2995 and family members $1995, based on two to a room. That covers 6 nights at a 4-star spa hotel, 4 full days of FIH-educator training, most meals, bike rentals for the week, two sightseeing days, and the Day 1 airport shuttle. Airfare is not included.

Is there a payment plan?

Yes. A $295 deposit holds your spot, then three payments spread across December 2026, March 2027, and May 2027. There's also a 72-hour grace period after registration for a full refund.

Where do we stay?

Hotel & Wellness Spa Zuiver, a 4-star wellness hotel in the Amsterdamse Bos on the edge of Amsterdam, right next to our training club and pitch. Over 140,000 square feet of spa and luxury facilities.

Who runs the training?

SportWays, Holland's internationally recognized hockey program founded by Thomas Tichelman. We train at their home club in Amsterdam with exclusive access to their top coaches, all FIH educators.

What do parents do while the athletes train?

Whatever you want. You'll have up to 8 hours of free time on training days: the hotel spa, biking into Amsterdam, the Rijksmuseum, the 9 Streets shops, or day trips to Delft or Volendam. This trip is built for you too.

Do we have to bike?

No, but it's the best part. Everyone gets a bike for the week, and the ride to training is 1.5 flat miles through a car-free park. Prefer not to ride? Walk or take an Uber.

How many athletes attend?

The tour is capped at 40 to 45 athletes to keep training groups small and the experience personal. Popular spots fill first, and rooms are assigned in booking order.
