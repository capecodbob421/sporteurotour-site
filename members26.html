Entry/Exit System (EES) — Schiphol Airport Arrival

Crossing the EU border at Schiphol with a non-EU passport.

European Entry/Exit System (EES): The Schengen area is collecting entry/exit data (biometrics like fingerprints/face) under the Entry/Exit System (EES); this is separate from visa rules and DOES NOT require prior application.

ETIAS (coming later): ETIAS will start operations in the last quarter of 2026. No action is required from travellers at this point. The European Union will inform about the specific date for the start of ETIAS several months prior to its launch. Once active, most U.S. travelers will need to apply online before entering any Schengen/associated country.

Upon arrival:

  1. Follow the signs: Walk towards passport control and follow the signs for "Non-EU Passports".

  2. Use the kiosk: Passengers aged 12 and older will be directed to an EES self-service kiosk. Scan your passport, have your photo taken, and provide fingerprints (usually four of your right hand).

  3. Pass through border control: After registering at the kiosk, you will be directed to either an automated eGate or a manned border control booth to finalize entry with an officer.

  4. Exceptions: Children under the age of 12 and passengers with specific disabilities cannot use the kiosks and must go directly to a staffed border control booth for assistance.

Helpful instructions:

Step 1. Put the passport on the upper glass panel; the lower is only for fingerprints. After putting the passport on the panel, the process will begin. Use only your right hand's four fingers, not the thumb. Press firmly against the glass (it is greasy and not wiped often). Keep the fingers together, not splayed apart. You may get a retry message here. Very important: DO NOT MOVE YOUR FINGERS WHILE IT SCANS. Hold them hard against the glass, fingers closed, zero movement. You will not see any light under them. Once it finishes, it will instruct you to follow route 1 or 2. One of these will force a human border guard check-in; the other will fast-track you to the actual entry machine.

Step 2. If you are fortunate, you get the machine track (it's supposedly faster). Feed your passport in as shown on the machine's diagram (it doesn't have the same glass panel as the first machine). Then it asks for fingerprints: same procedure. Right hand, 4 fingers, closed, pressed tight on glass. I FAILED THAT CHECK TWICE! Again, I think the glass is dirty, as I was very careful. But I refused to join the long human line. I tried a third time, very careful hand placement, zero movement, and SUCCESS! Alternatively, try swapping to the machine in the next aisle.

Once through immigration, you can go to the luggage carousel to collect your luggage, then proceed to the general terminal.

Dutch Specialties

Apple Cake (Dutch Appeltaart) — WINKEL 43: Noordermarkt 43, 1015 NA Amsterdam, in Amsterdam's ever-popular de Jordaan quarter. The crumb in this cake is light, flaky and moist. It is NOT a sweet cake. Served with whipped cream. They serve meals here too. Small venue with outdoor seating. Be careful where you sit; there's another cafe next to it.

Kroket/Croquet (or Bitterballen): Try CAFE PAR HASARD, located in one of Amsterdam's most beautiful neighborhoods on Ceintuurbaan 113-115, a 1.5 km walk south of the Rijksmuseum. Or EETSALON VAN DOBBEN near the Flower Market & Rembrandt Square, Korte Reguliersdwarsstraat 5-7-9.

If you are planning a trip to the Netherlands, there is one gastronomic delicacy you absolutely must try: the famous kroket. This Dutch delicacy is an irresistible croquette made from a combination of finely selected meats and enriched with a creamy roux, meat broth, spices and herbs. Eat it with mustard on white bread! But watch out, they're hot when first served. The Dutch version of pizza burn.

French Fries — Vlaams Friteshuis Vleminckx, the best Friteshuis: The best French fries can be found in the center of the city at Vlaams Friteshuis Vleminckx near the flower market and behind the main pedestrian shopping street, Kalverstraat 33, Amsterdam. Just fries! vleminckxdesausmeester.nl

De Frietfabriek (near our hotel): If you don't want to travel to the city, here's one very close to our hotel and training club: De Frietfabriek, Nieuwe Kalfjeslaan 1, 1181 CA Amstelveen. Excellent fries and snacks. defrietfabriek.com

Stroopwafel: If not fresh at a bakery, believe it or not, the best stroopwafel can be found at supermarkets.

Tulip Bulbs: There are two clearly marked kinds of bulbs for sale: those that can be imported to the US, and those that cannot. Some may claim that their bulbs are legal to take to the USA. However, they must have a metal tag that shows up on a scanner.
